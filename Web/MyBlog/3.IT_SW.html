What is the Metaverse?

The Metaverse is a combination of multiple elements of technology,
including virtual reality, augmented reality and video where users "live" within a digital universe. Supporters of
the metaverse envision its users working, playing and staying connected with friends through everything from
concerts and conferences to virtual trips around to the world. ​

Current metaverse ambitions are centered on addressing technological limitations with modern virtual and
augmented reality devices, as well as expanding the use of metaverse spaces to business, education, and retail
applications. Numerous entertainment and social media companies have invested in metaverse-related research
and development. ​

In particular, Metaverse started to spread in the context of the commercialization of 5G with high-speed,
hyper-connectivity, and ultra-low latency and the COVID-19 pandemic that hit the world in 2020.
In other words, along with the commercialization of 5G, technologies that can implement virtual reality (VR),
augmented reality (AR), and mixed reality (MR) have developed. As the non-face-to-face and online trend
spreads due to the COVID-19 crisis, Metaverse is drawing attention.
